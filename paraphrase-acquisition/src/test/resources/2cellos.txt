'''2CELLOS''' (stylized '''2CΞLLOS''') are a Croatian cellist duo,<ref>[http://www.2cellos.com/biography/ 2Cellos biography] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161222203922/http://www.2cellos.com/biography/ |date=22 December 2016 }} "Young Croatian cellists Luka Sulic and Stjepan Hauser, together known as 2CELLOS,..."</ref> consisting of classically trained cellists, [[Luka Šulić]] and [[Stjepan Hauser]]. Signed to [[Sony Masterworks]] since 2011, they have released five albums. They play instrumental arrangements of well-known pop and rock songs, as well as classical and film music.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nacional.hr/clanak/105855/hrvatski-violoncelisti-potpisali-ugovor-sa-sony-music-entertainmentom |title=Hrvatski violončelisti potpisali ugovor sa Sony Music Entertainmentom |last=Koruga |first=Igor |date=14 April 2011 |work=[[Nacional (weekly)|Nacional]] |language=sr|access-date=10 June 2011 |trans-title=Slovenian - Croatian cellists sign contract with Sony Music Entertainment |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68pXekqvV?url=http://www.nacional.hr/clanak/105855/hrvatski-violoncelisti-potpisali-ugovor-sa-sony-music-entertainmentom |archive-date=1 July 2012}}</ref> The duo performs internationally and has been featured on several American television series including ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]'' and ''[[The Bachelor (U.S. TV series)|The Bachelor]]'' (Sean and Catherine's Wedding).<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.dailymotion.com/video/x1afmcl_sean-lowe-catherine-giudici-wedding_people |title=Glee Season 18: Sean and Catherine's Wedding |access-date=26 December 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160115000120/http://www.dailymotion.com/video/x1afmcl_sean-lowe-catherine-giudici-wedding_people |archive-date=15 January 2016 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Background==
[[Stjepan Hauser|Hauser]] is from [[Pula]], [[Croatia]], and [[Luka Šulić|Šulić]] is from [[Maribor]], [[Slovenia]] (to Croatian father and Slovenian mother<ref name="Gloria">{{cite web |title=LUKA ŠULIĆ OTKRIVA KAKO MU JE BEZ HAUSERA I PROGOVARA O SVOJIM MANAMA: 'ZNAM JAKO BURNO I DIVLJE REAGIRATI' |url=https://www.gloria.hr/fokus/zvjezdane-staze/luka-sulic-otkriva-kako-mu-je-bez-hausera-i-progovara-o-svojim-manama-znam-jako-burno-i-divlje-reagirati/9911332/ |website=Gloria |access-date=27 September 2020}}</ref>). They met at a [[master class]] in Pula while still in their teens. They were both educated at the [[Academy of Music, University of Zagreb|Academy of Music]] in [[Zagreb]].<ref name="Luka Šulić official website">{{cite web |url=https://lukasulic.com/ |title=Luka Sulic - Official Website |website=Luka Šulić |access-date=27 September 2020}}</ref> Later on, Luka studied in [[Vienna]] and they both studied in the [[UK]] (Šulić at London's [[Royal Academy of Music]] and Hauser at Trinity College of Music, now [[Trinity Laban Conservatoire of Music and Dance|Trinity Laban Conservatoire of Music]] in London and then at the [[Royal Northern College of Music]] in Manchester).<ref name="Repubblica">{{cite news|url=http://www.repubblica.it/spettacoli-e-cultura/2011/06/06/news/il_violoncello_da_sogno_di_luka_e_stjepan_da_youtube_ai_concerti_di_elton_john-17259661/|title=Il violoncello da favola di Luka e Stjepan da YouTube ai concerti di Elton John|last=Videtti|first=Giuseppe|date=6 June 2011|work=la Repubblica|publisher=[[Gruppo Editoriale L'Espresso]]|language=it|access-date=10 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110609214952/http://www.repubblica.it/spettacoli-e-cultura/2011/06/06/news/il_violoncello_da_sogno_di_luka_e_stjepan_da_youtube_ai_concerti_di_elton_john-17259661/|archive-date=9 June 2011|url-status=live}}</ref>

The duo rose to fame in 2011 after their cover of "[[Smooth Criminal]]" became a hit on [[YouTube]],<ref name="STA">{{cite news|url=http://www.sta.si/en/vest.php?s=a&id=1637981|title=Slovenian, Croatian Cellists Make Breakthrough on YouTube|date=19 May 2011|work=STA|publisher=[[Slovenian Press Agency]]|access-date=10 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120407030857/http://www.sta.si/en/vest.php?s=a&id=1637981|archive-date=7 April 2012|url-status=live}}</ref> receiving over three million views in the first two weeks<ref name="Repubblica" /> and almost 43 million views as of April 2021.<ref name=Foot01/> The creation of the music video was assisted by one of Hauser's acquaintances in Croatia, a director, in creating a video to "Smooth Criminal." The video<ref name="Periodico">{{cite news|url=http://www.elperiodico.com/es/noticias/ocio-y-cultura/20110607/los-chelos-del-pop/1034351.shtml|title=Los chelos del pop|last=Cervera|first=Marty|date=7 June 2011|work=[[El Periódico de Catalunya]]|publisher=Grupo Zeta|language=es|access-date=10 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121010072815/http://www.elperiodico.com/es/noticias/ocio-y-cultura/20110607/los-chelos-del-pop/1034351.shtml|archive-date=10 October 2012|url-status=live}}</ref> features Šulić and Hauser facing off alone in a large ballroom littered with chairs, playing [[Michael Jackson|Jackson's]] tune.<ref name="Repubblica" /> Before they became partners, the two cellists were sometimes considered rivals, competing against each other in music competitions.<ref name="Periodico" />

==Career==
2CELLOS signed to record with [[Sony Masterworks]],<ref name="BWW"/> and finished recording their first album in May 2011,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.rtvslo.si/kultura/glasba/mariborski-celist-bo-z-eltonom-johnom-nastopil-tudi-v-izoli/257124|title=Mariborski čelist bo z Eltonom Johnom nastopil tudi v Izoli|date=9 May 2011|work=Radiotelevizija Slovenija|publisher=MMC RTV Slovenija|language=sl|access-date=10 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110513085703/http://www.rtvslo.si/kultura/glasba/mariborski-celist-bo-z-eltonom-johnom-nastopil-tudi-v-izoli/257124|archive-date=13 May 2011|url-status=live}}</ref> which carried the [[Menart Records]] label in Croatia.<ref name="MTV">{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com.hr/vijesti/2cellos-luka-sulic-stjepan-hausar-izdaju-album|title=2CELLOS Luka Šulic & Stjepan Hausar izdaju album|date=16 April 2011|work=[[MTV Adria|MTV Croatia]]|publisher=MTV|language=hr|access-date=10 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110620192355/http://mtv.com.hr/vijesti/2cellos-luka-sulic-stjepan-hausar-izdaju-album|archive-date=20 June 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> Released on 19 July 2011,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://blogs.phoenixnewtimes.com/uponsun/2011/06/welcome_to_the_jungle_as_perfo.php|title="Welcome to the Jungle," As Performed by 2CELLOS|last=D'Andrea|first=Niki|date=6 June 2011|work=[[Phoenix New Times]]|publisher=Phoenix New Times, LLC|access-date=10 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110612161032/http://blogs.phoenixnewtimes.com/uponsun/2011/06/welcome_to_the_jungle_as_perfo.php|archive-date=12 June 2011|url-status=live}}</ref> their debut ''[[2Cellos (album)|eponymous album]]'', includes covers of songs by [[U2]], [[Guns N' Roses]], [[Nine Inch Nails]], [[Sting (musician)|Sting]], [[Coldplay]], [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]], [[Muse (band)|Muse]], and [[Kings of Leon]]. The album also features a cello version of Michael Jackson's "[[Human Nature (Michael Jackson song)|Human Nature]]."<ref name="Repubblica" /> The duo said that they chose songs that could be arranged as covers playable on just two cellos.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://globus.jutarnji.hr/zivot/telefonski-poziv-koji-im-je-promijenio-zivot?onepage=1|title=Telefonski poziv koji im je promijenio život|last=Došen|first=Vanja|date=12 April 2011|work=[[Jutarnji list]]|publisher=[[Europapress Holding]]|language=hr|access-date=10 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110504083733/http://globus.jutarnji.hr/zivot/telefonski-poziv-koji-im-je-promijenio-zivot?onepage=1|archive-date=4 May 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> In anticipation of the release, 2CELLOS released their second single "Welcome to the Jungle," a cover of the Guns N' Roses song [[Welcome to the Jungle|of the same name]].<ref name="Periodico" /><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/06/08/2cellos-welcome-to-the-jungle_n_873416.html|title=2Cellos Covers 'Welcome to the Jungle'|date=8 June 2011|last=Hartsell|first=Carol|work=[[The Huffington Post]]|publisher=TheHuffingtonPost.com, Inc.|access-date=10 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110611015853/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/06/08/2cellos-welcome-to-the-jungle_n_873416.html|archive-date=11 June 2011|url-status=live}}</ref>

The duo performed an arrangement of "Smooth Criminal" in the third season of the television show [[Glee (TV series)|''Glee'']].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.tvguide.com/News/Kecks-Exclusives-Playlist-Glee-1041329.aspx|title=Keck's Exclusives: More Details on ''Glee''{{'s}} Salute to Michael Jackson|first=William|last=Keck|work=[[TV Guide]]|publisher=TV Guide Online Holdings, LLC|date=6 January 2012|access-date=6 January 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120108041840/http://www.tvguide.com/News/Kecks-Exclusives-Playlist-Glee-1041329.aspx|archive-date=8 January 2012|url-status=live}}</ref> The episode in which they appeared, titled [[Michael (Glee)|"Michael"]], aired on 31 January 2012, and was the first time that the series highlighted instrumental musicians. The scene featuring "Smooth Criminal" was set up similarly to 2CELLOS' original music video for the song.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052970204542404577156802762808124|title=The Cello Shredders|last=Jurgensen|first=John|date=13 January 2012|work=The Wall Street Journal|publisher=Dow Jones & Company, Inc.|access-date=1 February 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150103043228/http://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052970204542404577156802762808124|archive-date=3 January 2015|url-status=live}}</ref> On 4 June 2012 2CELLOS performed with Elton John at the Queen's [[Diamond Jubilee Concert]] at [[Buckingham Palace]]. On 12 June 2012, 2CELLOS performed in front of a full [[Arena Zagreb]] audience. The concert was filmed for DVD: [[Live at Arena Zagreb]].

Although the two usually record covers of rock songs, Šulić and Hauser said they still play with orchestras and will not abandon playing classical music. The cellists said they would someday like to go on tour with both a classical and a contemporary orchestra,<ref name="Periodico" /> a hope that was fulfilled in part during their tour for the album, ''Score''. Their second studio album, entitled ''[[In2ition (album)|In2ition]]'', came out early in 2013, and featured new twists on a diverse range of classic tracks with a few surprise vocal and instrumental duet partners including Elton John, [[Lang Lang]], [[Naya Rivera]], [[Steve Vai]], [[Sky Ferreira]] and [[Zucchero Fornaciari|Zucchero]]. The album was produced by [[Bob Ezrin]].

Other tracks include covers of [[AC/DC]]'s "[[Highway to Hell (song)|Highway to Hell]]" featuring Vai, [[Coldplay]]'s "[[Clocks (song)|Clocks]]" featuring Lang Lang, [[Muse (band)|Muse]]'s "[[Supermassive Black Hole (song)|Supermassive Black Hole]]" featuring Rivera, an Italian version of "The Book of Love" by [[The Magnetic Fields]] entitled "Il Libro Dell'Amore" featuring Zucchero, [[Karl Jenkins]]' "Benedictus", and "[[Oh Well (song)|Oh Well]]" featuring Sir Elton.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.2cellos.com/us/news/making-new-album-in2ition|title=The making of the new album IN2ITION|work=2Cellos Official Web Site|date=5 October 2012|access-date=21 October 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130205082225/http://www.2cellos.com/us/news/making-new-album-in2ition|archive-date=5 February 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In July 2013, they filmed a commercial for the Japanese mobile phone company [[NTT DoCoMo|Docomo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gloria.hr/vijesti/price/pogledajte-2cellos-u-reklami-japanskog-mobilnog-operatera/|title=Pogledajte 2Cellos u reklami japanskog mobilnog operatera|work=Gloria|language=hr|date=18 July 2013|access-date=2 August 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131220071728/http://www.gloria.hr/vijesti/price/pogledajte-2cellos-u-reklami-japanskog-mobilnog-operatera/|archive-date=20 December 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> Their album ''In2ition'' debuted on the [[Japanese Albums Chart]], in August 2013 at number 1.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/rank/wa/w/2013-09-02/|script-title=ja:2013年08月19日～2013年08月25日の洋楽アルバム週間ランキング|publisher=[[Oricon]]|language=ja|date=19–25 August 2013|access-date=6 September 2013|title=洋楽アルバム 週間ランキング-ORICON STYLE ランキング|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130929090816/http://www.oricon.co.jp/rank/wa/w/2013-09-02/|archive-date=29 September 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> Since then, they have done commercials for the US National Football League Thursday Night Football [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OPs9TEhnTxQ], for 1906 Reerva Especial [http://www.mundohr.com/11243-2/] and they have done several print campaigns for the Italian designer, [[s.Oliver]].

As a part of their world tour with Elton John, including concerts in North America, Europe and Asia, 2CELLOS performed at [[Madison Square Garden]] two days in a row, in December 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.24sata.hr/show/2cellos-su-osvojili-madison-square-garden-u-new-yorku-343963|title=2Cellos su osvojili Madison Square Garden u New Yorku|publisher=[[24sata (Croatia)|24sata]]|language=hr|date=4 December 2013|access-date=18 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131220163507/http://www.24sata.hr/show/2cellos-su-osvojili-madison-square-garden-u-new-yorku-343963|archive-date=20 December 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> In March 2014, they had a solo Japanese tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://radio.hrt.hr/clanak/2cellos-u-madison-square-gardenu/32110/|title=2Cellos u Madison Square Gardenu|publisher=Radio Pula|language=hr|date=3 December 2013|access-date=18 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131220111135/http://radio.hrt.hr/clanak/2cellos-u-madison-square-gardenu/32110/|archive-date=20 December 2013|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2015, 2CELLOS released their third album, ''[[Celloverse]]'', which they also co-produced.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/2cellos-create-a-new-dimension-with-celloverse-album-available-january-27-2015-300007198.html|title=2CELLOS Create A New Dimension With Celloverse Album Available January 27, 2015|last=Masterworks|first=Portrait/Sony Music|website=prnewswire.com|language=en|access-date=2017-02-05|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170923002845/http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/2cellos-create-a-new-dimension-with-celloverse-album-available-january-27-2015-300007198.html|archive-date=23 September 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> The album contains a diverse array of renditions including AC/DC's "[[Thunderstruck (song)|Thunderstruck]]", [[Avicii|Avicii's]] "[[Wake Me Up (Avicii song)|Wake Me Up]]", Michael Jackson's "[[They Don't Care About Us]]", [[Hans Zimmer]]ʼs "Mombasa" (from ''[[Inception]]''), and an original work titled ''Celloverse'', among numerous other covers.

[[File:2017 RiP - 2Cellos - by 2eight - 8SC1398.jpg|thumb|right|Drummer Dušan Kranjc (born in [[Pula]], [[Croatia]]) joined the group in 2011]]

2CELLOS has used YouTube to reach out to their audience, posting videos of notable live performances, including [[Arena Zagreb]] (2012), [[Pula Arena|Arena Pula]] (2013), [[EXIT Festival]] (2014), and their fifth anniversary concert at [[Arena di Verona]] (2016) in Italy. In February 2014, the duo published their rendition of [[AC/DC]]'s "[[Thunderstruck (song)|Thunderstruck]]" to YouTube. The video depicts the duo playing before a [[Baroque]] audience, which soon finds out that the song is anything but classical. The video went viral at the beginning of March, gathering 10 million views in the first two weeks.<ref name="Dnevnik.hr">{{cite web|url=https://dnevnik.hr/showbuzz/mladi_i_neizbjezni/2cellos-ruse-rekorde-spot-za-thunderstruck-pogledan-vise-od-10-milijuna-puta---326725.html|title=Naši dečki ruše rekorde – na Youtubeu imaju više 10 mil. pogleda!|publisher=Dnevnik.hr|language=hr|date=25 March 2014|access-date=25 March 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140326025255/https://dnevnik.hr/showbuzz/mladi_i_neizbjezni/2cellos-ruse-rekorde-spot-za-thunderstruck-pogledan-vise-od-10-milijuna-puta---326725.html|archive-date=26 March 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> As of March 2021, the video has garnered over 200 million views.

As Šulić told ''[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]'', "we love AC/DC as much as [[Bach]]."  Hauser added, "both (AC/DC and Bach) are simple and convincing in what they do."<ref name="Dnevnik.hr"/><ref name=journal>{{cite web  |url=http://digitaljournal.com/article/342499  |title=2Cellos offer fresh takes on old favorites  |access-date=14 May 2014  |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141023023602/http://www.digitaljournal.com/article/342499  |archive-date=23 October 2014  |url-status=live  }}</ref>

In 2017, Šulić and Hauser released their fourth album ''[[Score (2Cellos album)|Score]]'', recorded with the [[London Symphony Orchestra]] which included a wide array of [[Television program|television]] and [[film music]]. To mark the album's release, 2CELLOS released their official YouTube video for [[Game of Thrones]] Medley, which garnered over 1 million views in two days.

On 26 May 2018, 2CELLOS performed the [[UEFA Champions League Anthem]] ahead of the [[2018 UEFA Champions League Final]] between [[Real Madrid C.F.|Real Madrid]] and [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]].<ref>{{cite web |publisher=UEFA Champions League |title=2Cellos to perform UEFA Champions League anthem in Kyiv |url=https://www.uefa.com/insideuefa/about-uefa/news/newsid=2559940.html |website=UEFA Champions League |access-date=25 March 2019 |date=18 May 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190410143058/https://www.uefa.com/insideuefa/about-uefa/news/newsid%3D2559940.html |archive-date=10 April 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> That same year, they released their fifth album ''Let There be Cello.''
